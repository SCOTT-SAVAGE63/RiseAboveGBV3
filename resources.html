Protection Orders

Immediate court protection from abuse. Available at any magistrate's court.

How to Get a Protection Order:

  1. Visit your nearest magistrate's court
  2. Complete application forms (assistance available)
  3. Interim order granted same day
  4. Final order issued within court process

Know Your Rights

Understanding your legal rights and protections against gender-based violence

Your Rights Under South African Law

Every person has the right to be free from all forms of violence. The law provides specific protections for survivors of gender-based violence.

üîí

Right to Safety

You have the right to live free from violence and fear. The police must protect you when you report abuse.

‚öñÔ∏è

Right to Justice

You have the right to report abuse to police and have your case investigated properly.

üè•

Right to Medical Care

You have the right to receive medical treatment, including forensic examination, after assault.

ü§ù

Right to Support

You have the right to counseling, legal aid, and other support services free of charge.

üìù

Right to Protection Orders

You have the right to apply for a protection order at any time, including after hours.

üîç

Right to Privacy

Your personal information and case details must be kept confidential by all service providers.

Safety Planning Guide

Practical steps to increase your safety in different situations

1

Secure Your Home

  • Change locks if abuser has keys
  • Install security gates and cameras
  • Keep windows and doors locked
  • Install peepholes in doors
2

Create Safe Spaces

  • Identify safe rooms with locks
  • Keep phone charged in safe room
  • Plan escape routes from each room
  • Teach children emergency procedures
3

Emergency Preparedness

  • Keep emergency bag ready
  • Save emergency numbers on speed dial
  • Inform trusted neighbors
  • Practice emergency scenarios
1

Workplace Security

  • Inform security about protection order
  • Provide photo of abuser to security
  • Vary your arrival/departure times
  • Use different entrances/exits
2

Communication Safety

  • Screen calls at reception
  • Use work email cautiously
  • Keep personal information private
  • Inform HR about your situation
3

Travel Safety

  • Park in well-lit areas
  • Walk with colleagues to your car
  • Have security escort if needed
  • Keep phone charged and accessible
1

Online Privacy

  • Change all passwords regularly
  • Use two-factor authentication
  • Check for spyware on devices
  • Adjust social media privacy settings
2

Communication Safety

  • Use encrypted messaging apps
  • Block harassing numbers/accounts
  • Save evidence of harassment
  • Be cautious with location sharing
3

Device Security

  • Enable remote wipe features
  • Use screen locks with strong passwords
  • Log out of accounts when not using
  • Keep software updated
